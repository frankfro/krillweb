I started playing guitar when I was 18, quite late really. I spent my younger years as a competitive surfer whereby I made 5 national titles.

When I was about 16 I suffered a bad knee dislocation, which pretty much ended my surfing career. At 18 I bought a classical guitar and a how to play classical guitar book.

I spent hours in my room learning pieces by Sor and Bach. I made my own version of Bach's Bouree. As adolescence hit I discovered the electric guitar and became enthralled by the ability to play long solo's and improvise. I also started to compose songs in the popular and alternate genre.

There came a point where I was forced to take up a career as a Software Engineer. This made music take a back seat, and providing for my young family became a priority.

Forward 20 years and I have an 9 year old son who picked up classical guitar very quickly, he shows so much talent, in less than 2 years he was accepted to study an ANU University Music Program for High School Students. After my son attended a Summer Strings Camp for Classical String players, it sparked a renewed motivation in me and I started to compose classical music again.

Through those years I had played and composed in an ad hock fashion. I had also played around with a cheap DAW, but now I had a new passion, a new enthusiasm through my son's new talent.

I've now invested in the professional equipment required to produce top quality stock music for TV and Film. My music always holds a strong melody out front with dramatic moving themes and harmony.
